The Office

“The Office” is an American mockumentary sitcom that depicts the everyday work lives of office employees at the Scranton, Pennsylvania, branch of the fictional Dunder Mifflin Paper Company. The series covers nine years of footage as they find themselves recorded through their work days and off times.

The show follows the misadventures of a Dunder Mifflin Paper Company branch in Scranton, Pennsylvania, led by their unconventional and clueless boss, Michael Scott. The series is a mockumentary on a group of typical office workers, where the workday consists of ego clashes, inappropriate behavior, and tedium.

It aired on NBC from March 24, 2005, to May 16, 2013, with a total of nine seasons consisting of 201 episodes. The show was created by Greg Daniels, a veteran writer for Saturday Night Live, King of the Hill, and The Simpsons. It was co-produced by Daniels’ Deedle-Dee Productions and Reveille Productions (later Shine America), in association with Universal Television. The original executive producers were Daniels, Ricky Gervais, Stephen Merchant, Howard Klein and Ben Silverman, with numerous others being promoted in later seasons.
